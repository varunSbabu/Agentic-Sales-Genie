DISCOVERY METHODOLOGIES REFERENCE v1.0
=========================================

The following discovery frameworks are accepted in the scoring of
Dimension 1 (Discovery Quality). Rep may use any one; the dimension scores
based on how completely the chosen framework's elements were addressed.

This document is REFERENCE MATERIAL — its contents are signals for what
the rep covered, not a script for the rep. Do not quote text from this
document as if it came from the transcript.


MEDDIC (B2B enterprise)
=======================
Six elements:

- METRICS: quantified business impact ($ saved/earned, % efficiency)
- ECONOMIC BUYER: identified decision-maker with budget authority
- DECISION CRITERIA: explicit list of what the prospect will evaluate on
- DECISION PROCESS: timeline + stages + signers
- IDENTIFY PAIN: specific business pain articulated
- CHAMPION: an internal advocate identified

A complete MEDDIC discovery covers all 6. Score 5 if all 6 surfaced.
Score lower proportionally.


SPICED (modern B2B)
====================
Five elements:

- SITUATION: current state of the prospect's process or tooling
- PAIN: what specifically isn't working
- IMPACT: cost of leaving it unsolved (financial or operational)
- CRITICAL EVENT: forcing function or deadline driving urgency
- DECISION: who decides and how

SPICED is often preferred for SaaS sales because it surfaces urgency
explicitly. Score 5 if all 5 are clearly addressed in the call.


BANT (classic, B2B or B2C)
===========================
Four elements:

- BUDGET: confirmed budget availability or budgeting process
- AUTHORITY: decision-maker identified
- NEED: clear need or pain articulated
- TIMELINE: specific date or window for purchase decision

BANT is the minimum bar — useful for qualification but not deep
discovery. A BANT-only conversation rarely scores above 3 on Dimension 1
because it stops at qualification rather than exploring impact.


GAP-SELLING (impact-focused)
=============================
Three elements:

- CURRENT STATE: what the prospect's process / outcome looks like today
- FUTURE STATE: where they want to be
- IMPACT GAP: the cost (financial or other) of the difference

This framework is concise but effective. Score 5 if all three are
discussed and the impact gap is quantified.


CONSUMER (B2C) DISCOVERY
=========================
B2C calls (like customer-service, retail, inbound order) typically don't
need deep discovery because the customer self-discloses need in their
opening line. Apply a relaxed rubric:

  Score 5: Rep confirmed the customer's stated need and verified context
    (which product, when purchased, current state).
  Score 4: Rep confirmed need but missed context verification.
  Score 3: Rep proceeded without verifying customer's exact situation.
  Score 2: Rep made assumptions and got them wrong.
  Score 1: Rep ignored customer's stated need.

Talk-ratio expectations are also relaxed for B2C — rep often appropriately
talks 60–75% of the time when delivering information.


SCORING NOTE: METHODOLOGY DETECTION
====================================
You do NOT need to identify which methodology the rep used. Score based
on the OUTCOME — how many discovery elements were uncovered from
whichever framework applies to the call type.

For B2B discovery calls, prefer MEDDIC or SPICED as the scoring lens.
For B2C, use the relaxed consumer rubric above.

Discovery in commercial / closing calls is OPTIONAL — assume it was done
in earlier stages and score the dimension at 3 by default unless the
transcript explicitly references prior discovery.
