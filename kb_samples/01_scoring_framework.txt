SALES CALL SCORING FRAMEWORK v1.0
==================================

PURPOSE
-------
This framework is applied by the Sales Genie agent to score recorded sales
calls. The agent must use ONLY the rubrics below — it must not invent new
criteria or rely on generic sales knowledge. Every score must be backed by
direct evidence quoted from the transcript.


CALL TYPE CLASSIFICATION (apply this BEFORE scoring)
----------------------------------------------------
Identify the call's primary intent. Use the dominant focus, not the opening
small talk.

- DISCOVERY: Rep is exploring fit. Conversation is centered on the prospect's
  current state, problems, and decision context. The prospect's company,
  team, and processes are being learned. No product demo or pricing.

- DEMO / SOLUTION REVIEW: Rep is walking through the product with the
  prospect's stated pain in mind. Value is being articulated against a
  defined problem. Discovery may continue lightly.

- TECHNICAL VALIDATION: Implementation details, security, integrations,
  infrastructure, or compliance are the dominant topics. Often involves an
  engineer, architect, or InfoSec stakeholder on the prospect side.

- COMMERCIAL / NEGOTIATION: Pricing, terms, contracting, or procurement is
  the dominant topic. Quote has been sent or is being negotiated.

- CLOSING: Final commitments, ROI confirmation, executive sign-off, signed
  agreement walkthrough.

- FOLLOW-UP / CHECK-IN: Between major milestones. No new artifact being
  discussed in depth; goal is to maintain momentum or unblock.


DEAL STAGING FRAMEWORK
----------------------
After classifying the call, infer which stage the deal sits in BEFORE the
call. Then label whether the call ADVANCED, STALLED, or CREATED RISK relative
to that stage.

- Stage 0 — UNQUALIFIED: No fit confirmed; no pain identified.
- Stage 1 — QUALIFIED OPPORTUNITY: Pain confirmed, decision criteria
  emerging, budget unconfirmed.
- Stage 2 — SOLUTION REVIEW: Product fit demonstrated, multiple stakeholders
  introduced, decision criteria explicit.
- Stage 3 — TECHNICAL VALIDATION: POC, security review, or integration test
  in progress.
- Stage 4 — COMMERCIAL NEGOTIATION: Quote sent, redlining contract,
  procurement engaged.
- Stage 5 — CLOSED-WON or CLOSED-LOST.

A call ADVANCED the stage if a concrete artifact, meeting, or commitment was
agreed that moves the deal toward the next stage.
A call STALLED if no forward motion was committed.
A call CREATED RISK if new blockers emerged (budget gap, lost champion,
competitive replacement, fit issue).


SCORING DIMENSIONS (5 dimensions, each scored 1–5)
==================================================

DIMENSION 1: DISCOVERY QUALITY  (weight 25%)
--------------------------------------------
The depth and rigor with which the rep explored the prospect's situation.

A complete discovery covers SEVEN elements:
  1. Current state — how things work today
  2. Pain — what is specifically broken or limiting
  3. Quantified impact — dollars, hours, headcount, or percentage
  4. Timeline — when is this expected to be solved by
  5. Prior solutions — what they have tried already
  6. Decision criteria — what makes one solution better than another
  7. Decision process — who decides, in what order, by when

Scoring:
- 5 (Excellent): All 7 elements explored, with 7+ open-ended questions and at
  least one explicit quantification (numeric impact).
- 4 (Strong): 5–6 elements covered; quantification present but partial.
- 3 (Adequate): 3–4 elements covered; pain identified but impact not
  quantified.
- 2 (Weak): 1–2 elements covered; surface-level questions only.
- 1 (Poor): Rep launched into demo or pitch without discovery.

Evidence required: direct quotes of the open-ended questions asked, plus the
prospect quotes that did or did not quantify pain.


DIMENSION 2: ACTIVE LISTENING  (weight 15%)
-------------------------------------------
Whether the rep engaged with what the prospect said vs. waiting for their
turn to talk.

Markers:
- Paraphrasing the prospect's words ("So what I'm hearing is...")
- Clarifying follow-ups on prospect statements
- Acknowledging objections explicitly before responding
- Not talking over the prospect
- Reflecting emotion when prospect expresses frustration or urgency

Scoring:
- 5: Rep paraphrased 3+ times; asked clarifying follow-ups; never talked
  over the prospect; acknowledged at least one objection explicitly.
- 4: 1–2 paraphrases; mostly responsive; minor talking-over.
- 3: Relevant responses but no paraphrasing or reflection.
- 2: Some non-sequiturs, missed objections, or interruptions.
- 1: Rep ignored objections, talked over prospect repeatedly, or pivoted
  away from prospect's stated concern.

Evidence required: paraphrase quotes (or absence thereof) and any explicit
prospect concern that was ignored or addressed.


DIMENSION 3: VALUE ARTICULATION  (weight 20%)
---------------------------------------------
The rep's ability to tie product capabilities to the prospect's specific
stated pain — not a generic feature pitch.

Scoring:
- 5: Every benefit named was tied to a specific prospect quote. Example:
  "You said your team manually pulls a CSV every Monday — here's how our
  automation removes that." 4+ such linkages.
- 4: Most benefits tied to pain. 1–2 generic claims allowed.
- 3: Mix of pain-linked and generic claims. ~50/50.
- 2: Mostly generic pitching, with rare prospect-specific tie-ins.
- 1: Pure feature-dump with no connection to prospect context.

Evidence required: at least 2 quoted (prospect-pain → rep-benefit) pairings,
or note their absence.


DIMENSION 4: NEXT STEP CLARITY  (weight 25%)
--------------------------------------------
Whether the call ended with a specific, mutually confirmed next action.

A complete next step has FIVE elements:
  1. A specific date AND time
  2. A specific format (call, demo, in-person, written deliverable)
  3. A named attendee list
  4. A defined agenda or output
  5. Verbal confirmation from the prospect

Scoring:
- 5 (Excellent): All 5 elements present.
- 4 (Strong): 4 elements present; one is ambiguous (typically agenda or
  attendee list).
- 3 (Adequate): Specific date and time, prospect verbally agreed, but other
  elements vague.
- 2 (Weak): Vague "let's talk again next week" or "I'll send something".
- 1 (Poor): Rep promised to follow up with no prospect commitment, or no
  next step proposed at all.

Evidence required: the exact closing exchange where the next step was set.
This is the highest-leverage moment of the call — quote it precisely.


DIMENSION 5: TALK RATIO DISCIPLINE  (weight 15%)
------------------------------------------------
Based on the transcript's measured talk_ratio_rep.

For DISCOVERY calls:
- 5: Rep talked 30–45% of the time.
- 4: Rep talked 45–55%.
- 3: Rep talked 55–65%.
- 2: Rep talked 65–75%.
- 1: Rep talked more than 75%.

For DEMO calls:
- 5: Rep talked 50–60% of the time.
- 4: Rep talked 60–70%.
- 3: Rep talked 70–75%.
- 2: Rep talked 75–80%.
- 1: Rep talked more than 80%.

For COMMERCIAL / NEGOTIATION calls:
- Talk ratio is not a primary signal here. Score 3 by default and rely on
  the other dimensions.


OVERALL SCORE BANDS
===================
The overall score is the weighted average of the five dimensions.

- 4.0–5.0  EXCELLENT — coaching example, share with the team.
- 3.0–3.9  SOLID — on track, minor improvements identified.
- 2.5–2.9  MIXED — risk of stalling without rework on specific dimensions.
- Below 2.5  INTERVENTION REQUIRED — pipeline risk significant; manager
  should listen and coach within 48 hours.


LOSS RISK CATEGORIES
====================
Flag any of these signals that appear in the call. Multiple can apply.

- NO_BUDGET_CONFIRMED: prospect cannot describe how purchase decisions are
  approved or the budget cycle is unclear.
- CHAMPION_ONLY: a single point of contact, no executive sponsor named.
- VAGUE_PAIN: prospect cannot quantify pain ($, %, hours, count).
- NO_DECISION_PROCESS: no timeline, no evaluation criteria, no named
  decision-makers.
- COMPETITOR_LOCKED_IN: prospect references competitor as already chosen
  or strongly favored.
- STATUS_QUO_BIAS: prospect repeatedly says "we manage today" or "we'll
  think about it" without committing.
- TIMELINE_DRIFT: prospect previously gave a timeline that has now slipped
  without an explained reason.


STRENGTHS AND IMPROVEMENTS
==========================
Per call, the agent must produce:

- 3–5 specific STRENGTHS — each is a concrete moment quoted from the
  transcript, not a generic compliment. Bad: "Good rapport." Good: "Rep
  paraphrased the prospect's quote about manual CSV pulls before responding
  with the automation pitch — strong listening."

- 3–5 specific IMPROVEMENTS — each cites the missed moment. Bad: "Could
  ask better questions." Good: "When prospect said 'we lose maybe 20% of
  new users', rep did not ask what that means in revenue terms — should
  have followed up with quantification."


NEXT-STEP QUALITY LABELS
========================
Apply one of:
- ADVANCED: clear advance secured (concrete next meeting / artifact).
- STALLED: no forward motion committed.
- CREATED_RISK: new blockers exposed during the call.


ALERT ROUTING (used by Phase 5 alert node)
==========================================
- overall_score < user.alert_threshold_low  →  alert_level = "intervention"
- overall_score >= user.alert_threshold_high →  alert_level = "coaching"
- otherwise  →  alert_level = "none"
