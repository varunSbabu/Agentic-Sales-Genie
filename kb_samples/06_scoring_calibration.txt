SCORING CALIBRATION ANCHORS v1.0
====================================

Purpose: reduce variance between scoring runs by anchoring each dimension's
1/3/5 levels to concrete, observable behavior. The agent should compare the
transcript against these anchors and only choose a score for which the call
matches the anchor's REQUIRED OBSERVATIONS.

These anchors describe REQUIRED OBSERVATIONS, not example phrases. Do NOT
quote any text from this document as evidence — every evidence quote must
come from the actual call transcript.


DIMENSION 1 — DISCOVERY QUALITY
================================

Score 1/5  (POOR)
  REQUIRED OBSERVATIONS:
    - Zero open-ended questions about the prospect's current state
    - No pain identified or pain stated only by prospect (not probed by rep)
    - Demo, pricing, or pitch begins within the first 2 rep turns
  EXAMPLES OF 1/5 BEHAVIOR (not phrases — patterns):
    - Rep moves immediately to product features
    - Rep asks only logistics questions (name, account number)
    - Rep ignores pain when prospect mentions it

Score 3/5  (ADEQUATE)
  REQUIRED OBSERVATIONS:
    - 2-4 open-ended discovery questions present
    - Pain identified and acknowledged by rep
    - No quantification of pain (no $, %, hours, headcount, etc.)
    - No timeline or decision-process question

Score 5/5  (EXCELLENT)
  REQUIRED OBSERVATIONS:
    - 5+ open-ended questions
    - At least one quantified pain statement from prospect ($ / % / hours)
    - Timeline question asked AND answered
    - Decision-process question asked (who else, how decided)
    - Optionally: prior-solutions question, success-criteria question

Score 2 or 4: interpolate. A call that meets MOST of the next-higher anchor's
required observations rounds UP. Otherwise rounds DOWN.


DIMENSION 2 — ACTIVE LISTENING
================================

Score 1/5  (POOR)
  REQUIRED OBSERVATIONS:
    - Rep talks over the prospect at least once
    - Rep ignores an explicit objection or concern
    - Rep pivots away from prospect's stated pain to a different topic

Score 3/5  (ADEQUATE)
  REQUIRED OBSERVATIONS:
    - Responses are relevant to what the prospect said
    - No paraphrasing or summarising back of prospect statements
    - No clarifying follow-ups on prospect statements
    - No explicit acknowledgment when prospect raises a concern

Score 5/5  (EXCELLENT)
  REQUIRED OBSERVATIONS:
    - Rep paraphrases prospect statements at least twice ("So what I'm hearing is...")
    - Rep asks clarifying follow-ups on prospect-introduced topics
    - When prospect raises an objection, rep explicitly names it before responding
    - Rep never talks over the prospect


DIMENSION 3 — VALUE ARTICULATION
================================

Score 1/5  (POOR)
  REQUIRED OBSERVATIONS:
    - Pure feature-dump with no connection to any prospect quote
    - Generic claims ("we're the leader", "we're trusted by Fortune 500")
    - No mention of the prospect's specific pain when pitching

Score 3/5  (ADEQUATE)
  REQUIRED OBSERVATIONS:
    - Some benefits mentioned with vague connection to prospect context
    - 50/50 mix of pain-linked and generic claims
    - Pain references are paraphrased rather than directly quoted

Score 5/5  (EXCELLENT)
  REQUIRED OBSERVATIONS:
    - Every benefit named is tied to a SPECIFIC prospect quote earlier in the call
    - At least 3 explicit (prospect-pain → rep-benefit) linkages
    - When prospect raised a concern, rep used a value tie-in rather than discount


DIMENSION 4 — NEXT STEP CLARITY
================================

Score 1/5  (POOR)
  REQUIRED OBSERVATIONS:
    - Call ends with no next step proposed, OR
    - Rep says "I'll follow up" with no prospect commitment

Score 3/5  (ADEQUATE)
  REQUIRED OBSERVATIONS:
    - A next step is proposed
    - Prospect agrees in principle
    - No specific date, time, attendees, or agenda

Score 5/5  (EXCELLENT)
  REQUIRED OBSERVATIONS:
    - Specific date AND time set (e.g. "Thursday at 2pm")
    - Format identified (call, demo, written deliverable)
    - Named attendees if multiple parties
    - Agenda or expected output stated
    - Prospect VERBALLY confirms ("Yes, that works", "I'll send the invite", etc.)


DIMENSION 5 — TALK RATIO DISCIPLINE
================================

Use the talk_ratio_rep metric (provided in call metadata) directly:

For DISCOVERY / CHECK-IN calls:
  Score 5/5: Rep talked 30-45% of total time
  Score 4/5: Rep talked 45-55%
  Score 3/5: Rep talked 55-65%
  Score 2/5: Rep talked 65-75%
  Score 1/5: Rep talked more than 75%

For DEMO / SOLUTION REVIEW calls:
  Score 5/5: Rep talked 50-60%
  Score 4/5: Rep talked 60-70%
  Score 3/5: Rep talked 70-80%
  Score 2/5: Rep talked 80-90%
  Score 1/5: Rep talked more than 90%

For COMMERCIAL / NEGOTIATION calls:
  Score 3/5 by default unless an extreme pattern is observed.

For SERVICE / INBOUND calls:
  Score 4/5 if rep talked 50-75% (information delivery is expected).
  Score 3/5 if rep talked >75% without checking customer understanding.
  Score 5/5 if rep balanced delivery with customer verification (paraphrased
    back, asked "does that work for you?").


OVERALL SCORE BANDS — CALIBRATION
====================================

EXCELLENT (4.0 - 5.0)
  - Hit 4+ of 5 dimensions at level 4-5
  - Worth using as a coaching example
  - Deal advanced with concrete next step
  - No major loss-risk flags

SOLID (3.0 - 3.9)
  - Hit 3 of 5 dimensions at level 4-5
  - Some clear improvement opportunities but call was net-positive
  - Deal advanced with adequate next step
  - At most 1-2 loss-risk flags

MIXED (2.5 - 2.9)
  - Mix of strong and weak dimensions
  - Deal may stall without specific rework
  - 2+ loss-risk flags OR weak next step

INTERVENTION REQUIRED (Below 2.5)
  - Multiple dimensions at level 1-2
  - Deal likely to stall or churn
  - Significant loss-risk profile
  - Manager should listen and coach within 48 hours


CONSISTENCY RULES
====================================
When uncertain between two scores (e.g. 3 vs 4):

1. List the REQUIRED OBSERVATIONS for both scores
2. Count how many are observed in THIS transcript
3. Pick the score where MORE required observations are met
4. If exactly equal, round DOWN — conservative scoring is better than
   over-scoring

When a dimension's score would be 0/5 (no signal at all):
   - For Discovery calls: this is valid for non-discovery dimensions if the
     call hasn't progressed there yet
   - For Demo / later calls: 0/5 is rare; use 1/5 if the rep neglected the
     dimension entirely


EVIDENCE REQUIREMENT REMINDER
====================================
Every score must cite at least one evidence quote from the actual call
transcript. If no evidence exists in the transcript for a dimension, score
that dimension conservatively (1 or 2) — do not invent evidence.

The anchors in this document describe BEHAVIORS to look for in the
transcript, not phrases to copy. Quoting from this document into the
evidence field is forbidden.
